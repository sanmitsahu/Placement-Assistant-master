<p><a class="accordion-trigger ques" id="ques0"><strong>Question 1. Explain What Is An Operational Amplifier?</strong></a></p>
<p><span class="ans"><strong>Answer :</strong></span><p>An operational amplifier, abbreviated as op-amp, is basically a multi-stage, very high gain, direct-coupled, negative feedback amplifier that uses voltage shunt feedback to provide a stabilized voltage gain.</p></p>
</li><br><br><li class="quesans mb-20">
<p><a class="accordion-trigger ques" id="ques1"><strong>Question 2. State Assumptions Made For Analyzing Ideal Op-amp?</strong></a></p>
<p><span class="ans"><strong>Answer :</strong></span><p><strong>Assumptions made for analyzing ideal op-amp are:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Infinite open-loop gain</li>
<li>Infinite input impedance</li>
<li>Zero output impedance</li>
<li>Perfect balance</li>
<li>Infinite frequency bandwidth</li>
<li>Infinite slew rate</li>
<li>Infinite common-mode rejection ratio</li>
<li>Nil drift of characteristics with temperature</li>
</ul></p>
</li><br><br><li class="quesans mb-20">
<p><a class="accordion-trigger ques" id="ques2"><strong>Question 3. Explain What Are Differential Gain And Common-mode Gain Of A Differential Amplifier?</strong></a></p>
<p><span class="ans"><strong>Answer :</strong></span><p>When the difference of the two inputs applied to the two terminals of a differential amplifier is amplified, the resultant gain is termed as differential gain. But when the two input terminals are connected to the same input source then the gain established by the differential amplifier is called the common mode gain.</p></p>
</li><br><br><li class="quesans mb-20">
<p><a class="accordion-trigger ques" id="ques3"><strong>Question 4. Define Cmrr?</strong></a></p>
<p><span class="ans"><strong>Answer :</strong></span><p>CMRR is defined as the ratio of differential voltage gain to common-mode voltage gain and it is given as CMRR = Ad/Acm.</p></p>
</li><br><br><li class="quesans mb-20">
<p><a class="accordion-trigger ques" id="ques4"><strong>Question 5. Explain Why Does An Op-amp Have High Cmrr?</strong></a></p>
<p><span class="ans"><strong>Answer :</strong></span><p>High CMRR ensures that the common mode signals such as noise are rejected successfully and the output voltage is proportional only to the differential input voltage.</p></p>
</li><br><br><li class="quesans mb-20">
<p><a class="accordion-trigger ques" id="ques5"><strong>Question 6. Explain Why Open-loop Op-amp Configurations Are Not Used In Linear Applications?</strong></a></p>
<p><span class="ans"><strong>Answer :</strong></span><p>When an op-amp is operated in the open-loop configuration, the output either goes to positive saturation or negative saturation levels or switches between positive and negative saturation levels and thus clips the output above these levels. So open-loop op-amp configurations are not used in linear applications.</p></p>
